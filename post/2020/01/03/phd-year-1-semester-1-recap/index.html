PhD Year 1 Semester 1 Recap

JCS

2020/01/03

What a wild ride this semester has been! It’s 2:03AM on 1/3/2020. Considering what has happened in the past two hours, everything I’ve done seems a bit trivial. But we press on.

I’m most proud of arcospy, a python translation of an existing R package. More info here. The TLDR is that I built a python API that allows users to query the Drug Enforcement Agency (DEA) ARCOS opioid dataset for 2006-2012. Most of the heavylifting was carried out by the data investigator team at the Washington Post. Links to the repository and their original stories found here. It’s pip-installable! Try it out for yourself: pip install arcospy

During the 2020 winter term I will be the teaching assitant for GEOG306: Introduction to Quantitative Methods for the Geographic Environmental Sciences. It’s an online course with 13 or so students, and the first class went off without too much trouble. Even after a few years of really ‘doing R’ it is always nice to return to the basics and refresh our skills. I learn so many intuitive, base R tricks that I have forgotten. It makes one feel like a novice - but that’s not necessarily a bad thing! The feeling keeps us on our toes.

Looking forward to the 2020 Spring term - I may be taking both a machine learning (AOSC647) and bayesian methods course (SURV798B). I’m excited as it will be a chance ‘get under the hood’ of several commonly used machine learning algorithms, as well as a finally spend some dedicated time to understanding bayesian approaches.

Liking the new streamlined site! Simple, efficient, purposeful code.
