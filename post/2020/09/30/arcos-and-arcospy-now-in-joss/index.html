arcos and arcospy now in JOSS!

JCS

2020/09/30

Just a short post to announce a milestone in the arcos and arcospy journey! A writeup of the software was accepted at The Journal of Open Source Software. The paper is available online here. This was quite the adventure, and I thought it would be fun to quickly lay out the major moments leading up to this point.

  • Summer 2019: I read a Washington Post story about the release of a large portion of the ARCOS database and an accompanying software package to access the data called arcos. I mentally bookmark the data knowing that it could be relevant to my future research projects.
  • September 2019: I start as a PhD student at the University of Maryland College Park, and enroll in Dr. Taylor Oshan’s Models and Methods for Spatial Data Science course. As part of the class project I decide to translate the R arcos package to Python. This would be my first large Python endeavor. I get in contact with Mr. Andrew Ba Tran of The Washington Post to get an unofficial blessing for the project.
  • November 2019: Initial builds of arcospy make it onto PyPI.
  • December 2019 to April 2020: I work to improve arcospy with documentation, continuous integration testing, and ensuring that all the functions align exactly with arcos. At the suggestion of Dr. Oshan, I prepare a submission to JOSS detailing the cross-functionality of arcos and arcospy.
  • May 2020: Submit for pre-review at JOSS. Will remain under review until publication in September! Back-and-forth with two excellent reviewers to improve the presentation and functionality of the software.
  • September 2020: Submission is accepted and goes live on JOSS!

This is a great example of a full project lifecycle taking just over a year, even for a relatively straightforward project (not to mention all the time spent by members at The Washington Post). An excellent reminder to enjoy the journey rather than becoming anxious over the end result. arcos and arcospy offer an astounding amount of data and I hope that more researchers have a chance to explore it all in the future.
